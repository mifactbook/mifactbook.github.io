Tower

Rebuilt Tower

Wow! This thing is tall, tall, tall. Well, it's not as tall as a Kongo Mongo Tree but it'd still hurt to fall off the thing. Rebuilt Towers are the home of Wizards. If the Tower you are seeing is inhabited, a Wizard will be along shortly to inform you of your status. If it is empty, you can always go through the motions of attracting one of your own, if you know how.

Attracting A Wizard to Your Tower

If you ever manage to complete the rebuilding of a Tower, you will find it to be a relatively large pile of stone with lots of room to sit. You can even take turns spitting off the parapet. Ho-hum. What you really need to do is to attract the Tower's original Wizard who was banished at the end of the last Demigod War.

So while you still have your six group members, someone must issue the CALL WIZARD Order (CW). Your group will set about using the Xanxu Construct to spin around the Tower. Eventually, it will attract the wanted attention and, with a puff of smoke, will be magically transformed before your eyes. It will cost the monster who issues the order 85 Action Points.

Admit Groups to Your Tower

Being a Tower Master has its advantages and its responsibilities. Determining who gets to take advantage of the offerings is a big one. To approve a Group for admittance to your Tower, issue the ADMIT GROUP Order (AG <Tower#> <Group#>) where Tower# is the tower where you are Tower Master and Group# is the group you wish to grant admission to the Tower.

Admit Monsters to Your Tower

Okay, so you hate group X but like Monster Y. You want to keep out the riffraff but allow an individual to take part in the Wonderful World of Wizards. Easy enough, just use the ADMIT MONSTER Order (AM <Tower#> <Monster#>) where Tower# is the number of the Tower where you are the Tower Master and Monster# is the number of the monster you wish to grant entrance to.

Buying From and Selling To Wizards

Yep, you can even buy and sell at a Tower. Purchasable items are listed in the Tower service list and can be bought using the TOWER PURCHASE Order (P#) where # refers to the item listing on the services board. To Sell to a Tower, you must issue the same old Sell order you use elsewhere (P#) where # is the item number. Of course, Wizards don't buy everything. You can also collect bounties at Towers by issuing the COLLECT BOUNTY order (CB). Bounty items are also listed on the Tower Service List.

Dopplegangers

As if one version of a Ruk wasn't one version too many, Wizards have the remarkable talent of creating nearly exact duplicates of any living creatures. Dopplegangers are exactly that - carbon copies, right down to armor and clothing. They even do chores like cleaning and can be convinced to take baths with the touch of a staff. All Ruks should be so compliant. For what it is worth, Dopplegangers can be used for defenses and can fight just like the real item.

Special Skill Training at a Tower

With Wizards being an intelligent lot, you can learn special skills and knowledge at a Tower. To purchase such skill, issue the SPECIAL SKILL TRAINING ORDER (TK#) where # is the item you wish to learn about on the Tower's service list. It will cost you 80 Action Points to issue this order.

Spell Training at a Tower

Wizards know lots of things and are willing to trade this knowledge for a few oculars. If you wish to learn spells from a Wizard then issue the TOWER MAGIC Order (TX #) where # is the number referred to on the list of services offered. It will cost you 80 Action Points to issue this order.

Staying at a Tower

Much like an Inn, you can stay in a Tower 'tween turns and take advantage of the atmosphere and vapours for your betterment. To take up a room, issue the TOWER STAY order (TI <Level>) when you end your turn in the square with a Tower inhabited by a Wizard. Tower Stay levels are 1 (lowest), 3 (midrange), 5 (upper midrange) and 7 (the best).

Tower Master

Just what a Ruk needs, something to think about. Being a Tower Master is a big duty. You have to be the one to tell the Wizard who gets to come in and who gets to take a hike. At first, you might think this is a stupid chore, but once you learn more, you'll find it to be quite the little enterprise.

Tower Teleport

The presence of the major dieties is scarce in the Zargnoth, thanks to the work of the four major demi-gods. As such getting around can be a major drag. what with all that walking, and walking, and walking. That is unless you find a train and like being locked in a box for ages.

The Zoingots, being smarter than the average Ruk (but then, well, you know) had their Wizards work on this and the result is Telportation between Towers. To go from one tower to another, just issue the order, TT <Tower#>, where Tower# is the Tower you wish to go to. It'll cost you 100 oculars and 100 Action Points.
