For the elite Monster Island adventurer, having a Mount is a must. Riding a well-trained Mount gives you a great feeling of confidence and superiority. Plus the height advantage allows you to spot the smaller structures and terrain hazards quicker. To capture a Mount, you'll need at least a Jute Rope. Use the CAPTURE order. After capture, to begin riding and training your Mount, issue the TRAIN/WORK MOUNT order: TW. It uses 8 Action Points and shouldn't be done more than twice a turn (preferably at beginning and end). Don't overwork your Mount!

Note: The first time you Train/Work your Mount, it takes 24 Action Points and you'll expend your Jute Rope by making it into a crude harness. To release your current Mount, capture another Mount.

- |

Riding Skill Levels

Upon riding your first Mount, you become a Novice rider. Slowly you will improve and eventually attain the higer skill levels: Fair, Advanced, and Superior. Plodders are the best Mount to learn on because they're the most co-operative. However, it's unlikely that you'll attain that the Advanced skill level by riding a Plodder. Other Mounts are terrible to start on (hopeless might be a better word). With each advancement in skill level additional skills and/or abilities are received.

- |

Riding Lessons at a Far Inn

Your first look at the inside of the stables is when you first arrive at a Far Inn riding a Mount. Usually, one of the Jossmen at the Far Inn offers to teach, for a fee of 11 oculars, new riders how to handle, ride, and groom a Mount. Issue the order: X6. Additional riding lessons (at 7 oculars each) are available to novice riders. Again, issue the order: X6. Although Just men seem to be adept at riding a Mount, most Far Inns lack a very experienced rider. This is why you would only want to take about 5 or so additional lessons. Each lesson takes 30 Action Points. ---
